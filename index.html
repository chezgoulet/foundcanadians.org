02 / URGENT

Received a surrender letter? Here's what to do.

In June 2026, IRCC began sending letters to approved Found Canadians stating their citizenship certificates may be revoked and demanding surrender. This is a developing situation. Below is practical guidance — consult a qualified immigration lawyer for your specific case.

SURRENDER LETTER RESPONSE GUIDE

Important: A letter demanding surrender is not a revocation. You have rights and you have time. Do not panic. Do not act without advice.

1

Do not return your certificate yet

The letter asks you to. The standard timeline is 30 days. Use that time to get advice. Returning it voluntarily may waive your right to challenge the process.

2

Document everything

Keep the original letter, your application, your approval notice, your certificate number, and all correspondence with IRCC. Scan everything. Store copies in multiple places.

3

Get legal help

Contact an immigration lawyer experienced with citizenship matters. If cost is a barrier, reach out to the community — we are exploring mutual aid options. Organizations like Immigrant Quebec and Justice for Canadians may be able to help or refer.

4

Respond in writing

Acknowledge receipt of the letter. Request specific details about what documentation is allegedly missing or deficient. Ask for the review process in writing. Do not admit to any error or deficiency. A template is available from the community.

5

Share your story

If you received a letter, let the community know. The pattern of who is being targeted matters — age, location, documentation type, application date. The collective picture is stronger than any individual case.

03 / HOW WE WORK

Governed by consent, not command.

This is not a top-down organization. These are the principles the community has begun to articulate — they will evolve as more voices join.

01Community-owned

The domain, the resources, and any funds belong to the community, not any individual. Stewardship is a responsibility, not a power.

02Collaborative governance

Decisions are made by those they affect. No unilateral declarations. We build by consent and participation.

03Emergent and adaptive

We don't have a rigid structure. We're figuring it out as we go, responding to what the community actually needs. The form follows the function.

04Open source everything

Every resource we create is open and freely available. No paywalls, no gatekeeping, no proprietary claims on the path home.
